Cisco Network Security & VPN Lab

Routing & Switching · VLANs · OSPF · AAA · SSH Hardening · IPsec VPN

Overview

Built and secured a multi-router Cisco network to practise enterprise-style routing, segmentation, and secure remote connectivity. The goal was to configure a working topology, harden network devices, establish VPN connectivity, and validate end-to-end communication.

Lab Setup

  • Designed a multi-router / multi-switch topology with multiple internal subnets.
  • Segmented networks using VLANs and verified inter-VLAN routing.
  • Enabled dynamic routing (OSPF) to support scalable route advertisement and failover-style learning.

What I Did

  • Configured VLANs and trunk/access ports to enforce logical segmentation.
  • Implemented OSPF routing between routers and verified routing tables and adjacency behaviour.
  • Hardened device management by enabling SSH and applying baseline secure management settings.
  • Configured AAA-style access controls to manage administrative access and improve accountability.
  • Implemented site-to-site IPsec VPN connectivity and validated encrypted communication between networks.

What I Tested and Validated

  • Connectivity: verified end-to-end reachability across VLANs and routed networks.
  • Routing correctness: validated OSPF neighbour formation and correct route propagation.
  • Secure management: confirmed SSH-only administration and restricted access behaviour.
  • VPN functionality: validated traffic flow between sites and confirmed VPN status/establishment.

Key Outcomes

  • Strengthened understanding of practical routing, segmentation, and secure device management.
  • Improved confidence troubleshooting connectivity issues across switching, routing, and VPN layers.
  • Developed a structured approach to validating secure network builds (config → verify → test → document).

Tools

  • Cisco Packet Tracer / Cisco lab environment
  • Routers, switches (virtual topology)
  • OSPF, VLANs, AAA, SSH, IPsec VPN
